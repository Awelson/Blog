    今年一爆笑した炙りカルビゲームという神ゲーｗｗｗｗｗｗ　まふまふ・となりの坂田¶

    Transcript¶

    まふまふ：どうもうみなさんこんにちわ、まふまふです 

    Hello everyone, this is Mafumafu

    坂田：どうもこんにちわみなさん坂田です 

    Hello everyone, this is Sakata

    まふまふ：炙りカルビゲーム！ 

    It's the "Aburi Karubi" game

    This is a tounge-twister type game, the rules of which will be mentioned later on. 「炙りカルビ」 is a common tounge-twister in Japan. It means grilled korean ribs: 炙る meaning to grill and カルビ coming from the korean word: 갈비 (galbi) meaning ribs.

    坂田：なんだい？そのゲーム？ 

    What is that game?

    まふまふ：はい今日はでしけれども、炙りカルビというゲームをやっていこうと思います！ 

    So, this "Aburi Karubi" game is something I've heard about just today and I thought I wanted to give it a try

    まふまふ：こちらのゲームはですね～ 

    So about how this game works:

    ルール説明：

    • 噛みやすそうなお題を決めって 
      Decide on a tounge-twister

    "噛みやすい" literally means easy to chew and it is sometimes used with this intended meaning. However, "噛みやすそうなお題" does not mean "a phrase that seems easy to chew on", rather it is talking about a phrase that, when spoken, will likely make you bite your tounge, i.e, a tounge-twister.

    • 相手と自分で交互言い合って 
      You and your partner (the opponent) will take turns saying the decided phrase

    相手がうまく言い終えれば 

    If the opponent manages to say it correctly...

    回数を一回増やして自分が言う 

    The number of times you have to repeat the phrase increases by one

    それをキャチボールしていくゲム 

    It's just like catch ball!

    坂田：なるほどね～ 

    I see..

    まふまふ：例えば、僕は「炙りカルビ」って言ったら、さかたんが「炙りカルビｘ２」で、ばふばふ。。ばふばふ。。 

    For example, if I say "Aburi Karubi" then you should go "Aburi Karubi x 2" then Bafu-bafu.. Bafu-bafu (mispronounces his own name lol)

    坂田：で、ばふばふくんがｗ 

    then, Bafu-bafu will.. haha

    まふまふ：まふまふが。。まふまふが。。「炙りカルビｘ３」って言うっていうゲームなんですけど 

    I.. I should go "Aburi Karubi x 3", the game goes like that..

    坂田：なるほどなるほどでも。。すでに噛んでたけど、速攻終わりそうじゃない？俺だって滑舌めちゃくちゃ悪いぜ？ 

    I see I see.. but you failed already so shouldn't we call it quits already lol. I'm also bad with tounge-twisters too.

    「噛んで」which I translated to "failed", referring to the fact that mafu had already "twisted" (or bit (噛ん) in the japanese sense) his tounge by mispronouncing his own name earlier

    まふまふ：いや、さかたんも。。あんまいい方じゃないけど僕も大概だから 

    Yeah, I guess you are bad at tounge-twisters but I'm probably equally as bad

    坂田：じゃいい勝負できるかもな！ 

    Ah, then it's gonna be a fair match

    まふまふ：そう思うんだよ！一回「炙りカルビ」でやってみようか！ 

    That's what I thought too! Let's try "Aburi Karubi" to start with

    坂田：一回やってみよう！先攻取っていいすか私？ 

    Yeah, let's try that. Can I go first?

    まふまふ：あ、いいよ！ 

    Ah, go ahead

    坂田：じゃ先攻行きます、「炙りカルビ」 

    Then here I go.. "Aburi Karubi"

    まふまふ：「炙りカルビｘ２」 

    "Aburi Karubi" x2

    坂田：炙りカルビ　炙りツツ。。はっはっはｗｗｗ 

    Aburi Karubi, Aburi.. phww hahaha

    坂田：３本勝負にしてｗ、もっかいさせて！ 

    It only lasted three rounds haha, give me one more try

    まふまふ：早すぎい！ｗ 

    That was way too quick haha

    坂田：やっぱ先攻そっち！おれイヤだやっぱ。。 

    Forget about me.. maybe you should go first instead

    まふまふ：わかった、じゃ「炙りカルビ」じゃないやつやるよ 

    Alright, then let's do something else instead of "Aburi Karubi"

    坂田：あ！いや、もっかい「炙りカルビ」でやりたい！ 

    Ah, no let's do the "Aburi Karubi" one again

    まふまふ：何言ってんだよ！！ぬるい　ぬるいわ。。 

    What on earth are you saying, that's not cool

    ぬるい typically means lukewarm but in slang it means lame or not cool

    坂田：おれだけ噛んだらなんかイヤじゃん、恥ずかしいじゃんｗ 

    Only I failed earlier, its embarrassing

    まふまふ：いっぱいあるんだよ、いっぱい 

    Eh, but there's so many other tounge-twisters we have to go through

    坂田：もっかいｘ３「炙りカルビ」がもう１回やりたいんだ！ 

    One more x3 Let's do "Aburi Karubi" one more time

    まふまふ：うぜー 

    Ahhh how annoying

    うぜー probably originates from うるさい

    坂田：おれは「炙りカルビ」がやりてえんだ！！ 

    I don't care! I want to do "Aburi Karubi"

    まふまふ：わかったよ、しょうがないな 

    Alright, I get it..

    坂田：頼むわ！ 

    Please, I'm begging you

    まふまふ：じゃ行きますよ、「炙りカルビ」 

    Alright, here I go: "Aburi Karubi"

    坂田：「炙りカルビ」「炙るカルビ」 

    "Aburi Karubi" "Aburu Karubi"

    (Bursts of Laughter)

    坂田：違う話題いこっかｗ 

    So.. uh.. let's try a different one now

    まふまふ：ちょっと待って笑って喋れないｗ 

    Hold on, I can't stop laughing..

    まふまふ：次いくよ、次 

    Alright, let's move on now

    坂田：お願いします 

    Please..

    まふまふ：いくよ、「静岡塩漬け」 

    Alright.. "Shizuoka Shiozuke"

    Shizuoka (静岡) is a city in Japan while Shiozuke (塩漬け) means to heavily salt food as a way to preserve them -> link. It does not form any special meaning when you place them together, but because both of them sound similar it is a common japanese tounge twister when these words are said successively.

    坂田：静岡しず。。 

    "Shizuoka Shizu.."

    (Laughter)

    坂田：ダメだあ！！言えねえー！
